# UPS Maintenance Procedure

**Document Type:** Maintenance SOP
**Author:** George Amankwaa Sarpong
**Last Updated:** June 2026

---

## Purpose
Standardised procedure for planned UPS maintenance ensuring critical power protection is maintained throughout the maintenance window with zero impact to protected loads.

---

## Pre-Maintenance Requirements
- Change request approved minimum 5 business days in advance
- Maintenance window communicated to all stakeholders
- Backup power verified operational
- UPS vendor engineer confirmed on-site or available remotely
- All critical loads identified and risk assessed

---

## Safety Requirements
- NFPA 70E arc flash PPE required — minimum Category 2
- Lockout/Tagout procedure must be followed
- Two-person rule applies for all high voltage work
- Emergency contact numbers posted in work area
- First aid kit and fire extinguisher accessible

---

## Maintenance Procedure

### Phase 1 — Preparation (30 minutes before)
1. Verify backup generator is operational and fuelled
2. Test automatic transfer switch operation
3. Notify all stakeholders of maintenance start
4. Confirm UPS bypass path is available
5. Review UPS fault history for known issues

### Phase 2 — Transfer to Bypass
1. Verify load is within bypass rating
2. Initiate manual bypass transfer via UPS interface
3. Confirm load transferred to bypass successfully
4. Verify all protected loads still operational
5. Document bypass start time

### Phase 3 — Maintenance Activities
1. Apply lockout/tagout to UPS input
2. Verify zero energy with test equipment
3. Perform scheduled maintenance tasks:
   - Battery inspection and testing
   - Connection torque verification
   - Capacitor inspection
   - Fan cleaning and replacement if needed
   - Firmware update if required
4. Document all findings with photos

### Phase 4 — Return to Normal
1. Remove lockout/tagout devices
2. Restore UPS input power
3. Allow UPS to complete self-test
4. Verify battery charging commenced
5. Transfer load from bypass back to UPS
6. Confirm all monitoring alerts cleared
7. Document completion time

### Phase 5 — Post-Maintenance
1. Update maintenance log with findings
2. Raise work orders for any defects found
3. Update next maintenance date
4. Notify stakeholders of completion
5. Close change request
